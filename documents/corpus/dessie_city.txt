Dessie City — Home of Wollo University

Dessie is the capital city of South Wollo Zone and one of the major cities in the Amhara Region of Ethiopia. The city is located approximately 401 kilometers north of Addis Ababa. Dessie sits at an elevation of about 2,470 meters above sea level, giving it a cool and pleasant climate throughout the year.

History:
Dessie was founded in the late 19th century by Emperor Yohannes IV. The city grew rapidly during the reign of Emperor Menelik II and became an important administrative and commercial center in northern Ethiopia. During the Italian occupation of Ethiopia (1936-1941), Dessie served as a regional capital.

Population:
Dessie has a population of approximately 300,000 people, making it one of the largest cities in Ethiopia outside of Addis Ababa. The city is home to people from various ethnic groups including Amhara, Oromo, Tigrinya, and others.

Economy:
The economy of Dessie is based on trade, services, and small-scale manufacturing. The city is an important commercial hub for the surrounding agricultural areas. Major products traded in Dessie include grain, livestock, coffee, and manufactured goods. The presence of Wollo University has contributed significantly to the local economy by creating jobs and attracting students and visitors from across Ethiopia.

Education:
Dessie is home to several educational institutions including Wollo University, Dessie Institute of Technology, and numerous primary and secondary schools. The city has a relatively high literacy rate compared to other cities in the region.

Healthcare:
Dessie Referral Hospital is the main hospital in the city and serves as a teaching hospital for Wollo University's College of Medicine and Health Sciences. The hospital provides specialized medical services to patients from across the Wollo region.

Transportation:
Dessie is connected to Addis Ababa and other major cities by the main north-south highway. The city has a bus station that serves as a hub for transportation to surrounding towns and villages. There is also an airport near Dessie that provides air connections to Addis Ababa.

Culture:
Dessie has a rich cultural heritage. The city is known for its traditional music, food, and festivals. The annual Timkat (Epiphany) and Meskel (Finding of the True Cross) celebrations are major cultural events in Dessie.
