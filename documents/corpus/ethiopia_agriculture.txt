Agriculture in Ethiopia

Agriculture is the backbone of the Ethiopian economy, accounting for about 36% of GDP and 85% of exports. The sector employs about 73% of the labor force. Ethiopia is one of the world's oldest agricultural societies.

Major Crops:

1. Coffee
Ethiopia is the birthplace of coffee. Coffee is the most important export crop, accounting for about 30% of export earnings. The main coffee-growing regions are Kaffa, Sidama, Yirgacheffe, and Harrar.

2. Teff
Teff is a grain native to Ethiopia and is the main ingredient in injera, the traditional Ethiopian flatbread. Ethiopia is the world's largest producer of teff. Teff is highly nutritious and gluten-free.

3. Wheat and Barley
Wheat and barley are important cereal crops grown in the Ethiopian highlands. Ethiopia is one of the largest wheat producers in sub-Saharan Africa.

4. Sorghum and Maize
Sorghum and maize are important food crops grown in the lowland areas of Ethiopia. They are staple foods for millions of Ethiopians.

5. Pulses
Ethiopia is a major producer of pulses including chickpeas, lentils, and faba beans. Pulses are important both for domestic consumption and export.

Challenges in Ethiopian Agriculture:
- Dependence on rain-fed agriculture
- Land degradation and soil erosion
- Limited use of improved seeds and fertilizers
- Poor rural infrastructure
- Climate change and drought

The Ethiopian government has been implementing various programs to modernize agriculture and improve food security, including the Agricultural Transformation Agency (ATA) and various irrigation development programs.
